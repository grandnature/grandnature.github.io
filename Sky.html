The Sky


The daytime sky is dominated by the sun, the scattered sunlight which gives the sky its blue colour, and a rich variety of clouds. At night, it is transformed and we see the stars, planets and moon, as well as man-made satellites and, on occasion, shooting stars (meteors). In between, we have the glories of the sunrise and the sunset.
 
The sky has always fascinated mankind, and it should also fascinate children once their attention is drawn to what it has to offer.

  • The Sun and Moon dominate the sky, crossing it from east to west and exerting a profound influence on life on Earth.
  • The weather is famously changeable in the UK, so it provides plenty to notice and understand.
  • Stars are a source of wonder, and this wonder is only increased by knowing a little about what you are seeing.
  • One of the most striking and beautiful sights in Nature is a rainbow. These provide an opportunity for children's art and for some scientific understanding.
  • Start a Nature Journal - a place for children to draw and write about their encounters with Nature.
