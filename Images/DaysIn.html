Days in


There are still plenty of Nature-related activities which can be done indoors when the weather doesn't inspire a trip outside. It's a good opportunity for artwork, scientific experiments or talking to children about the world to explore and develop their understanding.

  • Start a Nature Journal - a place for children to draw and write about their encounters with Nature.
  • Grow your own bean or pea plant in a jar to watch first hand how seeds germinate and grow.
  • Small natural objects such as leaves, flowers, twigs, stones and shells can be used to make a Nature collage.
  • Leafprinting is fun and will draw children's attention to the different shapes of leaves and their vein structure.
  • Sun prints are a rather magical way of producing detailed silhouettes of natural objects such as leaves, flowers, seed heads and feathers.
  • One of the most striking and beautiful sights in Nature is a rainbow. Showery days provide the best chance to see one, and you can even make your own indoors.
  • Small creatures collected from the surface layers of the soil can be kept indoors for a few days and studied.
